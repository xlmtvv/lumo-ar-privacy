Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 10, 2026

LUMO AR ("we", "our", or "the app") is an augmented reality flashcard application developed by Alisher Mutaliyev as an individual developer. Your privacy matters, and this policy explains what information we collect, why, and how we handle it.

1. Information We Collect

Data Purpose Storage
Email address Account creation & authentication Firebase Auth
Display name Personalization (via Google Sign-In) Firebase Auth
Flashcard content Creating and studying flashcards On-device only
Location (when in use) Saving AR flashcard positions in the real world On-device only
Camera feed Rendering augmented reality experience Not stored
AR world map data Restoring AR card positions between sessions On-device only

We do not collect analytics, use advertising SDKs, or track you across apps or websites. The app does not use Apple's Ad Tracking (IDFA) and sets NSPrivacyTracking to false.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • Authentication: Your email and name are used solely to create and manage your account via Firebase Authentication.
  • AI-Generated Answers: When you use the "AI Generate" feature, the text of your flashcard question is sent to our secure server to generate an answer. Only the question text is transmitted — no personal data, device identifiers, or location data is included in this request.
  • Location: Your GPS coordinates are used exclusively on-device to anchor flashcards in augmented reality. Location data is stored locally in Core Data on your device and is never transmitted to any server.
  • Camera: The camera feed is processed in real-time by ARKit for augmented reality rendering. No images or video are recorded, stored, or transmitted.

3. Third-Party Services

These services receive only the minimum data necessary for their function. We do not share your personal data with any other third parties for marketing, advertising, or data mining purposes.

4. Data Storage & Security

  • On-device data (flashcards, decks, AR world maps, location coordinates) is stored locally using Core Data and is protected by iOS data protection.
  • Authentication data (email, display name) is stored by Firebase Authentication in accordance with Google's security practices.
  • All network communication uses HTTPS/TLS encryption.
  • We do not maintain our own external database of user content.

5. Data Retention & Deletion

  • On-device data: Deleted immediately when you delete the app or remove content within the app.
  • Firebase Auth account: You can delete your account at any time through the Profile section in the app. This removes your authentication record from Firebase.
  • AI requests: Flashcard text sent for AI generation is processed in real time and is not stored by our server. OpenAI's data retention policies apply to their processing; refer to their privacy policy linked above.

To request complete deletion of all data associated with your account, contact us at the email address listed in the Support section below.

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you may also exercise rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), including the right to data portability and the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.

Common Questions

How do I delete my account?

Open the app → go to the Profile tab → tap "Delete Account". This will remove your authentication data from Firebase. On-device data (flashcards, decks) is deleted when you uninstall the app.

The AR feature isn't working

Make sure you've granted camera and location permissions in Settings → LUMO AR. AR requires a device with an A12 chip or later running iOS 17.0+. Ensure you are in a well-lit environment.

Can I use the app without location access?

Yes. Location is only used for saving AR card positions. You can use all other features — creating decks, studying flashcards, AI generation, and quizzes — without granting location access.

Is my data sent to any server?

Only two things leave your device: (1) your email/name for authentication via Firebase, and (2) flashcard question text when you use the AI generate feature. Everything else — your flashcards, decks, AR data, and location — stays on your device.
